S1. 64. ताम्बूल &c., एला &c. – The words परिणद्ध and आलिङ्गन in
these two epithets are significant. They show how congenial the
southern land is to the growth of love; how even trees and creepers
there &re, as it were, joined in wedlock. The two epithets also show
how nature in the southern quarter holds forth all the luxuries
necessary to make lovers happy. तमाल &c. - This shows that the
ground in the southern quarter is naturally covered with a soft
covering. प्रसीद - be ‘pleased, ' ie if you will accept the king &5 your
husband and condescend to honour these beautiful sites with a visit,
the purpose
of their existence will be fulfilled. स्थली– A natural
spot of ground, as opposed to स्थला which is कृत्रिमा (touched by art) भू.
S1. 65. इन्दीवर - इन्दी लक्ष्मीस्तस्या वरं (liked by ) इन्दीवरं तदिव श्यामा
तनुर्यस्य. शरीरयष्टि - शरीरं यष्टिरिव a stick - like ie slim or thin body; an
excellent bodily frame; f. प्राप्ता तथा तानवमङ्गयाष्ट: Vik. Ch. IX. 38.
अन्योन्यशोभा &c. - अन्यस्य अन्यस्य अन्योन्यस्य; see note on परस्पराक्षिसादृश्यम्
I. 40; अन्योन्यस्य शोभा &c. तडित्तीयदयोः - According to Chár. this
gives the reason why the princess rejects the king, as the comparison
suggests a short-lived union.
S1. 66. लेभेऽन्तरं &c. - Anidiomsticexpression. दिवाकर - दिवा करो-
तीति be who rouses men to activity during the day; दिवा दिनं करोताति
वा the maker of the day; the sun. This refers to चेतसि also. In this
case दिवाकर means by उपचार, दिवाकरवंशज, i. e. Aja. Indumati's mind
had an intuitive longing for Aja and so Sunandá's words found no
room in her heart. Hem. also remarks to the same effect. अरविन्द
-- a day-lotus, अरं शीघ्रं लिप्सां (desire to possess ) विन्दति इति, अराकाराणि
पत्राणि विन्दति वा ।
Ś1. 67. संचारिणी - पुनः पुनः संचरतीति संचारोऽस्या अस्तीति वा संचारिणी,
applies to दीपशिखा by उपचार or लक्षणा, like कोशन्ति in the instance
माः कोशन्ति, and to Indumati. दीपशिखा - Indumati resembled the
flame of & lamp by her lustrous beauty. Cf. स्वं रोचनागौरशरीरयष्टिः 61, 65.
यं यं स स – i e every one, whoever was passed over became pale.
